Choosing the Right Model

For Quick Questions

Simple queries, definitions, basic coding help

  • Recommended: GPT-5-nano (Flex)
  • Cost: ~$0.0001 per message
  • Speed: Very fast
  • Example: "What's the capital of France?" or "Fix this syntax error"

For Complex Tasks

Writing, analysis, coding projects, creative work

  • Recommended: GPT-5 or GPT-4o
  • Cost: $0.01 - $0.20 per message
  • Speed: Fast
  • Example: "Write a business plan" or "Analyze this data"

For Math & Logic

Mathematical problems, logical reasoning, step-by-step solutions

  • Recommended: o3 (Flex) or o4-mini
  • Cost: $0.01 - $0.50 per message
  • Speed: Slower (thinks through problems)
  • Example: "Solve this calculus problem" or "Debug this algorithm"

For Research-Level Work

PhD-level problems, complex research, advanced reasoning

  • Recommended: o3-pro (Large effort)
  • Cost: $1.00 - $5.00+ per message
  • Speed: Very slow (deep thinking)
  • Example: "Prove this theorem" or "Design a complex system"

Understanding Context Windows & Tokens

What Are Tokens?

Tokens are pieces of text that AI models process. Understanding tokens helps you control costs.

  • 1 token ≈ 4 characters or about 0.75 words
  • 100 tokens ≈ 75 words (one paragraph)
  • 1,000 tokens ≈ 750 words (one page)
  • Both your input AND the AI's response count as tokens

Context Windows Explained

Context is how much conversation history the AI remembers. More context = better continuity but higher costs.

  • Short context (2K tokens): Last few messages only
  • Medium context (8K tokens): Several pages of conversation
  • Long context (32K tokens): Entire conversation history
  • Maximum (128K tokens): Hundreds of pages of context

Advanced Settings

Temperature

Controls how creative or focused the AI's responses are.

  • Low (0.1-0.3): Focused, consistent, factual responses
  • Medium (0.7): Balanced creativity and accuracy
  • High (1.0-1.5): Creative, varied, sometimes unexpected
  • Best for most tasks: 0.7 (default)

Effort Level (O-Models)

How much time the AI spends thinking through problems.

  • Small: Quick thinking, lower cost, faster responses
  • Medium: Balanced thinking time and cost
  • Large: Deep thinking, higher cost, best quality
  • Choose based on: Problem complexity and time sensitivity
